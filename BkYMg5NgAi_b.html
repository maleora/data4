First off I love Mary's style of writing. Have to say I loved Hudson from the beginning, He has a great take charge attitude. This book is full of beautiful descriptions of the island and of Miguel. It makes me want to hop a plane to visit ASAP. This is a quick story with not a lot of backround in the characters but in this case I don't... Not my favorite, but still really good, easy reading. Classic Mary Calmes. :) Hudson is stranded (yes, there is some suspension of belief) on Ibiza by his ditzy twin brother. After despairing over his MBA studies, he starts turning his brother's (ex-)boyfriend's businesses around, which then catches the attention of said boyfriend.... I found this repetitive, and while I know that this is partially the charm of Mary Calmes, it just didn't work for me.I wanted to slap both twins, a lot, and Miguel seemed like a caricature of a Spanish hero, and no one really felt real.
